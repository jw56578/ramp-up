Tu Quoque
Illicit major
Texas Sharpshooter

The tu quoque fallacy is committed when it is assumed that because someone else has done a thing there is nothing wrong with doing it.

Illicit major is a formal fallacy committed in a categorical syllogism that is invalid because its major term is undistributed in the major premise but distributed in the conclusion.

The fallacy's name comes from a parable in which a Texan fires his gun at the side of a barn, paints a bullseye around the bullet hole, and claims to be a sharpshooter.
